Product Teardown

Swiggy's Missing
Feature: Multi-Restaurant
Orders

By Prithvik T Hanson    Grade 10    Aspiring Product Manager    May 2026
Overview

What is Swiggy?

Swiggy is India's leading food delivery platform. Millions of users, thousands of restaurants, one goal: get food to your door fast. It does that job well. But there's a gap that nobody's talking about.

What happens when you want food from two different restaurants at the same time?

The Problem

You can't order from two restaurants at once.

Picture a family ordering dinner. One person wants KFC, another wants pizza from Domino's. On Swiggy today, that means two separate orders, two separate delivery fees, and two deliveries arriving at completely different times. The food gets cold. Everyone's annoyed. It shouldn't be this hard.

This isn't a rare edge case. It happens at every family dinner, every house party, every office lunch where people have different preferences. That's a lot of frustrated users.

Who this actually affects:

Families ordering dinner House parties and gatherings Office group orders Couples with different cravings Friend groups
Why It Doesn't Exist Yet

The logistics problem is real.

This feature hasn't been built because it creates a genuine operational mess. One driver picking up from two restaurants has to go to the first spot, wait, drive to the second, wait again, then head to the user. That's a longer wait, colder food, and a more expensive operation for Swiggy.

Swiggy's entire model is built around speed. One restaurant, one driver, one delivery. Multi-restaurant orders break that model.

But the problem is too real to keep ignoring. Here's how I'd solve it without killing the speed promise.

My Solution

Two options at checkout. User picks what works for them.

When someone tries to order from multiple restaurants, Swiggy gives them a simple choice at checkout:

OPTION 01

Single Driver Route (Cheaper, Slower)

One driver picks up from both restaurants and delivers everything together. Costs a bit more, around a 10 to 15% service fee increase, but it's the affordable option. Good for people who don't mind waiting a little longer.

OPTION 02

Parallel Delivery (Faster, Premium)

Two drivers pick up from each restaurant at the same time and arrive together. Food comes hot. It costs roughly double the delivery fee, but Swiggy One members could get occasional free parallel delivery as a perk. That alone gives people a reason to subscribe.

Tradeoffs

What this would cost Swiggy.

Upside

Solves a real, frequent pain point for groups and families ordering together.

Risk

Adds significant complexity to Swiggy's logistics and routing system.

Upside

The premium parallel option creates a new revenue stream and strengthens the Swiggy One value proposition.

Risk

The single driver route could lead to food quality complaints if timing between pickups is off.

Upside

No food delivery platform offers this today. It would be a real differentiator against Zomato.

Risk

Driver pay structure would need reworking to fairly compensate multi-stop routes.

Existing Feature Gap

Swiggy's Group Order doesn't solve this.

Swiggy already has a Group Order feature, but it's basically just a shared QR code so friends can add items to the same basket from the same restaurant. It doesn't let you order from multiple restaurants at once.

Multi-restaurant ordering would be a natural extension of Group Order. Combine both and you have a proper "ordering together" experience that actually works.

Impact

Why this matters.

Food delivery is fundamentally a social activity. People order together. When a platform forces everyone to compromise or manage multiple separate orders, it creates friction at the exact moment the experience should feel seamless.

This feature doesn't just fix a gap. It makes Swiggy the platform that actually understands how people eat together.
